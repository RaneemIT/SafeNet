Lesson 2

Authentication

How does authentication protect accounts?

Authentication protects accounts by checking identity before access is allowed. It blocks unauthorized users and reduces the chance of stolen accounts, data leaks, and online fraud. As digital services grow, secure login methods become more important for both individuals and organizations.

Why is authentication important?

Authentication helps make sure that only authorized users can enter systems. It protects personal privacy, supports secure communication, and reduces risks linked to weak or stolen credentials.

Key Benefits of Authentication

🔐
Protects user accounts from unauthorized access
✅
Verifies the identity of real users
🛡️
Strengthens security when combined with MFA

Where is authentication applied?

Authentication is used in websites, mobile apps, banking platforms, school systems, and workplace services. It is required anywhere users need secure access to digital resources.

Examples of Authentication Use

🏦
Banks use login verification for accounts and payments
🎓
Schools protect student portals and course records
💼
Companies secure email, files, and internal systems
