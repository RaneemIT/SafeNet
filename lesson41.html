Lesson 4

Types of Malware – Part 2

Advanced Malware Types

In addition to viruses, worms, and trojans, there are more advanced types of malware that focus on spying, locking data, or hiding deeply inside a system. These threats are often harder to detect and can cause serious financial and operational damage.

Understanding advanced malware helps users and organizations recognize how attackers steal information, interrupt services, and maintain hidden access to infected devices. Strong security awareness is essential to reduce these risks.

Key Advanced Malware Types

Some malware is designed not just to spread, but to stay hidden, spy on user activity, or demand money. These forms are especially dangerous because they often target sensitive information and critical systems.

Advanced Malware Categories:

  • Spyware
    • Monitors user activity secretly
    • Collects browsing and personal data
    • May steal usernames and passwords
  • Ransomware
    • Encrypts files or locks devices
    • Demands payment for recovery
    • Can stop normal business operations
  • Adware
    • Displays unwanted advertisements
    • Tracks user behavior
    • May reduce device performance

Advanced Threat Comparison

Type Description Potential Risk
Spyware Malware that secretly watches user activity and collects information. Privacy loss and credential theft
Ransomware Malware that encrypts files or blocks access until payment is made. Data loss and financial extortion
Adware Software that forces unwanted ads and may track behavior. Poor performance and privacy issues
Rootkit Malware that hides deep inside the operating system. Hard detection and long-term compromise
Keylogger Malware that records what users type on the keyboard. Password theft and account compromise
