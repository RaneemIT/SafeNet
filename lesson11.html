Lesson 1

Introduction to Cybersecurity

What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks, devices, and data from unauthorized access, attacks, damage, or theft. It helps individuals and organizations keep their digital information safe and maintain secure online services.

In today’s connected world, cybersecurity is essential because people rely on websites, cloud systems, mobile apps, and digital communication every day. A strong cybersecurity strategy reduces risks such as data breaches, malware infections, phishing attacks, and identity theft.

Why do we use it?

Cybersecurity is used to protect sensitive information, maintain privacy, and ensure that systems continue to work safely and efficiently. Without cybersecurity, digital systems become vulnerable to hackers, fraud, and service disruption.

Types of Multi-Level Lists Generated:

  • First level
    • Protect personal accounts and passwords
    • Prevent malware and phishing attacks
    • Secure online communication
  • Second level
    • Protect business data and customer records
    • Reduce financial and operational risks
    • Support safe remote work and cloud usage
  • Third level
    • Improve trust in digital services
    • Meet security policies and compliance needs
    • Strengthen overall digital resilience

Common Cyber Threats

Threat Description Potential Risk
Malware Malicious software such as viruses, worms, and trojans. Data theft and system damage
Phishing Fake emails or messages designed to steal personal data. Identity theft and account compromise
Ransomware Malware that locks files and demands payment. Data loss and financial extortion
DoS Attack Overloading a service to make it unavailable. Service downtime and lost revenue
Password Attack Attempts to guess, steal, or crack passwords. Unauthorized access to systems
