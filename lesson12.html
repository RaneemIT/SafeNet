Lesson 1

Introduction to Cybersecurity

Where does cybersecurity come from?

Cybersecurity emerged as a response to the growing dependence on computers, digital networks, and online communication. As technology developed, so did the risks of unauthorized access, data theft, malware, and cyberattacks. Today, cybersecurity has become a core part of protecting personal data, business systems, and national digital infrastructure.

Why is cybersecurity important?

Cybersecurity helps protect users, devices, and organizations from attacks that can cause financial loss, privacy violations, and system damage. It supports safe communication, secure online services, and trust in digital environments.

Key Benefits of Cybersecurity

🔒
Protects passwords and personal accounts
💳
Reduces fraud and financial theft
🖥️
Prevents malware from damaging systems

Where is cybersecurity applied?

Cybersecurity is used in homes, schools, hospitals, companies, banks, and government systems. Any environment that stores data or connects to the internet needs security practices to reduce risks and maintain safe access.

Examples of Cybersecurity Applications

🏢
Businesses protect customer and company data
🏥
Hospitals secure patient records and devices
🏛️
Governments secure critical digital infrastructure
