Lesson 2

Authentication

What is Authentication?

Authentication is the process of verifying that a user, device, or system is really who or what it claims to be. It is one of the most important security steps in protecting digital accounts and sensitive information.

Every time users log in to email, banking apps, or learning platforms, authentication helps prevent unauthorized access. Strong authentication methods reduce the risk of account theft and improve trust in online systems.

Why do we use it?

Authentication is used to confirm identity before granting access to accounts, files, or services. Without it, anyone could try to enter systems and misuse private or valuable information.

Types of Authentication Factors:

  • Something you know
    • Passwords
    • PIN codes
    • Security questions
  • Something you have
    • Smartphones
    • Security tokens
    • One-time passcode apps
  • Something you are
    • Fingerprint recognition
    • Face recognition
    • Voice verification

Common Authentication Methods

Method Description Main Benefit
Password A secret word or phrase used to log in. Simple and widely used
PINA short numeric code often used on devices. Fast access
Biometrics Uses physical traits like fingerprints or face scans. Harder to copy
OTP A temporary code sent by app, email, or SMS. Extra layer of protection
MFA Combines two or more authentication factors. Strong account security
