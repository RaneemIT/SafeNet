Lesson 4

Types of Malware – Part 2

How can advanced malware be prevented?

Advanced malware can be reduced through strong security practices such as updating software, using antivirus tools, avoiding suspicious links, and training users to recognize unsafe downloads and phishing attempts. Early detection and regular backups also help reduce the damage of infections.

Why is advanced malware dangerous?

Advanced malware is dangerous because it can hide from basic protection, spy on users for long periods, or block access to important data. These threats often target sensitive systems and may cause financial, technical, and privacy-related harm.

Key Risks of Advanced Malware

🔐
Encrypts valuable data and blocks access
👁️
Hides from basic security tools
🕵️
Enables long-term spying on users and systems

Where is advanced malware used by attackers?

Attackers use advanced malware against personal devices, business servers, and high-value digital systems. Their goal is often to steal information, interrupt operations, or maintain secret access for future attacks.

Common Targets

💻
Personal computers and laptops
🏢
Business servers and shared networks
🏦
Financial and administrative systems
